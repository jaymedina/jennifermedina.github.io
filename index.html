Jennifer V. Medina

Welcome! I am a Research & Instrument Analyst at the Space Telescope Science Institute with an interest in the planetary sciences, software development, data analysis, web development, and web design. My background is primarily software development, and astronomy (particularly data calibration and instrumentation). Outside of work, my interests include outreach to promote an interest in STEM among youths and minorities, as well as salsa dancing, hiking, kayaking, and photography.

Wide Field Camera 3 (WFC3)

I work as a Research & Instrument Analyst matrixed into the WFC3 instrument branch to service the Hubble Space Telescope. My responsibilities include developing software for processing incoming WFC3 data (calibration and/or science), and conducting explorative analysis on this processed data. Many of my current projects involve working with calibration files of the UVIS (Ultraviolet and Visible wavelengths) detector, such as the un-flashed and post-flashed dark reference file projects, but I also dabble in UVIS photometry.

Exoplanet Characterization Tool Kit (ExoCTK)

The Exoplanet Characterization Tool Kit (ExoCTK) is an open-source software package meant to aid users with JWST proposal writing and exoplanet atmospheric characterization. My work in ExoCTK involves enhancing and maintaining the Contamination Overlap tool which is one of ExoCTK's observation planning tools. Feel free to play around with this tool, and others, on the ExoCTK website.

Previous Work

Extrasolar Systems in Praesepe

In the summer of 2016, I participated in the TAURUS summer research program in the University of Texas, Austin, where I worked with Dr. Andrew Mann and his team to calculate Vsin(i) values of several young planet-hosting stars in the Praesepe star cluster. The project involved developing software in python that used forward modelling techniques to determine the Vsin(i) of a target star from its spectra. My poster can be viewed here. This project was part of Dr. Mann's fourth ZEIT publication, which can be viewed here.
